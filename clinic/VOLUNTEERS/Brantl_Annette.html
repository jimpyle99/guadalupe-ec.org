Volunteer Experiences

Volunteer Experiences.........

How to work as a volunteer at the clinic in Guadalupe. My experience working here at least two months.

The first thing, which I did not expect, was the fact that the patients have to pay, at least no less, for the treatments. This is very important for the quality of the work. For example, I didn´t make a root canal filling with just one “gutta-percha point”. I did the normal lateral filling. This was very important for me to try to do the same quality work I do in Germany.

We just do, in Guadalupe, root canal filling on teeth with one canal. I think that is at least very good, because when you see how destroyed the teeth are which need a root canal filling you also have to make a crown. Otherwise it just does not work out. Crowns we cannot do here, so it is much cheaper and more comfortable for the patients to pull the tooth out directly.

The patients we see here are very different. There are ones that have money, coming with their own cars from far away and on the other side the poor people where some do not even have a tooth brush.

Many patients, at least with money, know exactly what they want. You have to choose it, what they need. Some, with really bad teeth and bad behavior (not brushing teeth) just want composite fillings for the aesthetics. Especially on the molars and with patients with high risk carries they need the antibacterial action of amalgam. Sometimes people will not understand this and leave.

For me it was important to give a guarantee for my work at the clinic. The clinic also gives a guarantee to their patients.

Many times I found it better to make a control check by letting the patient brush their teeth by themselves. Then I can see if they do it right or I need to help them. For me, the prophylaxis is the most important thing. Many people just do not know how to brush their teeth, to use dental floss or interdental brushes. That is why we need more dental hygienists who want to show the people how to treat their teeth right.

Also it is very important to educate the patients about changing teeth. Most of them didn´t know that the milk teeth have to be alive till they fall out, and this is with 12 years of age (at least). Even the most educated do not recognize that the first molar (16, 26,36,46) is a permanent tooth because no milk tooth is falling out.

The clinic is well organized, and we dentists, have all we need. The only thing we don´t have is a panorama x-ray. That is why it is not allowed to pull out difficult wisdom teeth. I think for dentists with more experience than I have, just two years, it is different. I was at least glad that I did not need to pull difficult wisdom teeth. I pulled many others.

There we have the next problem. If you pull out teeth and make a denture, wait until the swelling is totally gone. Otherwise the denture does not have a perfect fit. It is very hard to explain to people that a “ lining” afterwards is necessary for a perfect fitting. So just leave that problem and make it later.
