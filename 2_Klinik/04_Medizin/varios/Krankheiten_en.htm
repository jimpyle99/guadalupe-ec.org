"....the common illnesses that we have here.... a lot of fungal infections, chronic cough, gastritis, chronic diarrhea, depression.  Those are probably the most common.  Actually, the list (above)  is fairly complete. 
I am trained as a pediatrician, so in terms of kids' problems, there is a lot of concern regarding growth, familial short stature, chest pain, chronic cough and diarrhea. 
In adults, I see hypertension, Type II diabetes, BPH, menopause, A LOT of musculoskeletal pain.  It is similar to the US with the addition of the occasional weird case of neurocystercercosis, congenital toxoplasmosis, tuberculosis......" (Dra. Beena Kamath, October 2003)

 

"...  a clinic in Ecuador based at a mission in the Oriente region along the Peruvian border. It serves a local indiginous population of 3,ooo but in turn draws from a wider provincial population of about 30,000. The clinic has examining rooms, two dental suites, an operating theater, and an opthalmology/optometry suite. A rather complete pharmacy is on premise. There is a laboratory currently performing cbc, fungal and gram stains, pregnancy tests, vaginal smears, stool parasites, and urinalyses. There is an arrangement for PAP smears to be read at a regional cancer hospital. Some commercial chemistry measurement is available at a laboratory about 2 hours bus ride away.
The patients suffer from a variety of illnesses but this includes diabetes and heart disease. It would be of great use to be able to assess lipid profiles in this setting. There are medications in stock to intervene and manage dyslipidemias...."
(Dr. Serle Epstein, November 2003)
