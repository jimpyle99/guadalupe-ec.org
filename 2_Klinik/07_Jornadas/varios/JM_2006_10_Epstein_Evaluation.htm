JORNADA MEDICA  -   OBSERVATIONS

 October 2006

by Dr. Serle Epstein

 

As it currently stands, a Jornada, from a medical provider point of view, is very busy but much easier to run, focussed upon chief complaints rather than chronic illness and preventative care. This however is suboptimal. Ideally we would do much more of the practice of longitudinal care for chronic illness. Hopefully time would also be made for education services. This would entail charting, more regular visits to the pueblos, and either bringing more medications with us or promoting travel to the pharmacy in Guadalupe. As we do more, we would see fewer patients per health care provider. They would need more time. I think any venue greater than one day's travel away should be visited at least quarterly, and more effort should be made to have inclusion of the local health care personnel, particularly the government health center workers. It would be great if we could have Jornada Medicas every 3-4 months, on a regular circuit to underserved villages, and with a predictable amount of resources. We would need to recruit more personnel, perhaps utilizing academics or students as well as private practitioners. We would also need to either have a ready source of medications, or more efficiently, donate money to the clinic periodically for this purpose. They can buy discounted pharmaceuticals. We now have a foundation Friends of the Mission Clinic of Our Lady of Guadalupe, Inc. for such a project.
