JORNADA MEDICA  -   EVALUATION

 MARCH 23, 2004

 

Six medical personnel came from Washington state on Monday, March 15, 2004.  Two translators from Loja were available.  Dr. Beena Kamath and Nurse Amanda Anderson also accompanied the personnel from the brigade.  The Jornada Medica was located in the Province of Zamora.  Dr. Kamath had to return Monday, March 22, due to a medical emergency for one of the doctors who was working at the mission clinic in Guadalupe.  The following are notes taken during the evaluation session and are not listed in order of priority.

 

ü     Regarding standardized treatment for patients in this province, more information is necessary on the common ailments particular to the province prior to beginning work with patients.  Briefing provided by clinic medical personnel who have already worked with the people of the province would greatly help to prepare the volunteers.  

ü     A list of common diseases, the name of medications and appropriate strenghts would be helpful for the volunteers.  This would help to move patients along through the process and thus enabling the voluteers to see more patients in a day.

ü     Walkie talkie availability would increase the ease and speed of communication for the volunteers particularly between the medical personnel and the nurse distributing medications.

ü     The “ficha” system and medication perscriptions need a clear but discrete identifying symbol so that all medical personnel are aware when a patient has already been seen and/or received their perscription.

ü     Pre-written directions for medication and what it is being used for would ensure clarity for patients.  Sheets in plastic folders for volunteers to refer to with directions to chose from may be made available.  Pre-cut strips would be extremely helpful.  This would help to move along the process to allow medical personnel to see the greatest number of patients possible in a short span of time.

ü     Reducing the variety of meds that are brought with the medical brigade will simpify the process.

ü     Availability of Pedi bags to collect urine would be useful.

ü     Extra paper or pads for medical personnel will allow for private notes to be taken as well as handing out more lengthy medical information to a patient.  Personal observational notes of needs, suggestions for improvements or comments on what is really going well taken by volunteers throughout the Jornada Medica would be extremely helpful when the group is evaluating the experience on the last day.  Suggestions for personal items that volunteers have observed as being necessary can help to prepare the next group of volunteers.  A list could be made available to volunteers prior to their departure for the jornada.

ü     Information regarding the clinic such as the telephone number, hours of operation, and doctors (specialty if appropriate) who are due to arrive within the next weeks needs to be communicated to volunteer prior to the Jornada Medica.

ü     Pre-printed information for diabetes, cleanliness, use of unboiled water, etc. would be useful for both preventative and health education purposes.

ü     Health education that can be provided orally for patients may ensure better health for the community over time.  It must be concise and simple given time constraints.

ü     Accomodations were adequate and volunteers were also pleased with the quantity and quality of the food.  All were satisfied with the efforts of the organizers of the brigade from the barrios and the gratitude that was expressed by all community members was heartwarming.

ü     Volnteers loved the 10:30 coffee break.  That was better condition than has been experienced at home!

ü     Prior to leaving for the return trip to the US, a minimum of 24 hours should be available for a brief R&R moment.  A stay at the Guadalupe Mission was greatly apprecated by the volunteers, although it was extremely brief.  The accomodations at the mission are most comfortable with a nice opportunity for evaluative discussions, relaxation and rest.

ü     Volunteers should understand that many of the medical needs may appear routine, however, these are the greatest needs of many.  Addressing these seemingly uncomplicated medical needs is indeed a step toward “saving the world”. 

ü     It was greatly appreciated by Guadalupe Mission Clinic personnel that the medical brigade volunteers maintained high respect for the work of the clinic and encouraged the patients to go the clinic for follow-up visits as needed.

ü     The promotion of good health practices that was encouraged by volunteers was considered to be a support to the clinic’s philosophy.  Additionally, respect for the clinic’s philosophy to educate the public to boil water, to encourage proper diet and personal hygiene and cleanliness, to not give away medicine or other supplies for free by volunteers, etc. is essential to continue to promote good practices. 

ü     Contracts with barrio logistical coordinators should be in writing and written very specifically with information on charges for housing, food and amount expected in return for patient visits.

ü     Prior and extensive notification of a Jornada Medica’s arrival that is well publicized will maximize the number of patient visits.

ü     In one location, the coordinators sold appointments with a specific time to the people of the barrio.  This provided for a more orderly assemply of patients who came at a prescribed hour and who also had prepaid their “ficha”

ü     The “ficha”, ticket with a number designating place in line, should have chief complaint, blood pressure and age written clearly.

ü     Volunteers may work as long as a 16 hour day given unusual circumstances, although every effort is made to avoid such a long day.

ü     Approximately 10 volunteers saw 1,326 patients over a period of 9 days.

ü     A side trip to a gold mine was extremely appreciated by the volunteers.  Where possible, one side trip throughout the visit makes the experience even more powerful.

ü     Flexibility on the part of volunteers and all personnel who are involved with a Jornada Medica is necessay.  There are many variables that occur which may result in significant changes in the plan of the day.

ü     The coordinator of this Jornada Medica is enthusiastically prepared to conduct another session during the month of February 2005. 

 

As an observer who spent some time working with the volunteers in a barrio and was present for the evaluation meeting, I would like to note that these volunteers and the two clinic personnel were outstanding individuals dedicated to providing medical support for the people of this province.  The coordinator had a remarkable ability to maintain a sense of team spirit and uplifted morale in spite of an intense and demanding workload.  A strong leader with the support of caring, dedicated volunteers teamed with the leadership of Padre Jorge Nigsch, the organizer, the experienced Guadalupe Clinic personnel and the efforts of the people of the barrios coordinating the logistics makes for a brilliant success.  It is an experience that I shall never forget and will maitain the highest regard for each and every one of these individuals.         

                                                                      Respectfully Submitted,  

                                                                      Marie Salazar Glowski

                                                                      March 24, 2004
