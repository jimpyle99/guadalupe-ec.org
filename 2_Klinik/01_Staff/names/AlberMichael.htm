Experience - Erfahrungsbericht

 

I am Michael Alber, a general practitioner, normally working at Austria. I
stayed in Guadalupe for 7 weeks in sept.-nov. 2005. The clinic here and also
all the missional camp around  is a very nice and peaceful place and I will
keep this time in my mind as a very important and impressing part of my
life.
So, thankyou Padre Georg for setting all these things in the way they are
now!

The clinic is very well equiped with medicine from donations either from
Austria or the U.S., so nearly all groups of medicine are provided in our
pharmacy. We have also a little laboratory for some basics and ECG. The
clinic normally starts at 8 a.m. and the number of patients is changing from
day to day, but most of the times the mornings are busy. If you are lucky
there are days you can finish in the early afternoon and have a look outside
the clinic, take a walk to local communities or just relax on the balcony of
our residence with a very nice view over the valley!
 

Most common diseases are:

 

Intestinal parasites, Gastritis/GERD, Bronchitis, Asthma, few Tbc of lungs,
Skin problems, Gynecological disorders/STD (we do PAP-Exam), Diabetes mell.,
Systemic Hypertension, few Heart failures or Heart defects, Vitamin
deficiency and of course Osteoarthritis (lots of patients with back pain,
joint pain, muscle pain, burning feet and headache!).
 Another part of our work is selecting patients for the specialists who
arrive at this clinic from time to time.

Considering my work I sometimes worry a little bit about the following
problems:


- At the moment we have no possibility to detect liver diseases (if you are
a very experienced doctor you may detect them just by physical examination
and send them to exams to Loja, the next big town 3 hours away). So, during
my stay I met two patients after having a drainage of a "liver abscess".  I
think these things don't occur every day, but it seems from time to time
there are some of these problems. For the future it would be great if we had
one of our Ultrasound-Machines working and perhaps some kind of blood exam.


- There are some cases of Neurocysticercosis in this region (I met two
patients under treatment, which were detected by specialists in Loja). It
seems very hard to choose in between the hundreds of patients with headache
which one could have Neurocysticercosis. So, I think it's just worth to
think about this disease from time to time if you have a patient with very
well and clearly described neurologic symptoms or lately had severe pain
attacks. Another disease symptom would be the appearance of subcutaneous
nodes.  If you have a suspect the blood exam is available at Loja (I don't
know if it's the very specific EITB or the less specific ELISA test) and
also tomographies are available there if the patient has enough money.
It would also be worth discussing if we take Praziquantel from time to time
into our antiparasitic treatment to get a better coverage for tapeworms (its
not as expensive now as some years ago: see also
www.who.int/wormcontrol/en/). At the moment there is no Praziquantel
provided, we use Albendazol, Mebendazol, Tinnidazol and/or Metronidazol to
treat parasitic symptoms. I found one meta-study published in Cambridge
Parasitology Journal (2000) which says Albendazol 400mg for 3 days should
have a 75% coverage for tapeworms, while Praziquantel has 99%
eliminationrate in single-dose-use.


-During our medical brigade we met in the valley of Guaysimi some children
with a more or less amount of ascites and I wasn't sure about the origin.
Diseases I would think about were malabsorption (by Giardia lambliasis?),
protein deficiency in nutrition or infectious diseases (Tbc, Parasites,
Malaria, also Malaria is not very frequent in the region). One of these
children was reported to have fever from time to time. So I sent them to a
local pediatrician. If you meet these problems also on other Jornadas, it
would make sense to make more research on the reason or controlling those
kids.

At last I want to say thanks to my colleague Kerry Jones for advice during
work here, especially for helping me with some gynecological problems of my
patients, for preparing hot water for coffee breaks and all the energy and
humour you brought with you.

Books:

there are some nice books of internal, pediatric and tropical
medicine in the clinic (english).
For german-speaking people I can recommend for preparing the trip the
following ones:
-Praktische Tropen- und Reisemedizin (Diesfeld, Krause, Teichmann;
Thieme-Verlag; kurz, leicht, praxisorientiert)
-Tropen- und Reisemedizin (Knobloch; Gustav-Fischer-Verlag; viel
tropenmedizinisches Wissen, von einem erfahrenen Tropenmediziner sehr
plastisch aufgearbeitet und interessant zu lesen, auch wenn man die meisten
dieser Krankheiten hier in Guadalupe nie zu Gesicht bekommen wird)
-Reisemedizin (Kretschmer, Urban & Fischer Verlag; etwas praktischer
aufbereitet, auch gut fuer reisemedizinische Beratungen zu hause geeignet).

If there is need for special chemicals in the future: some are available at
Loja, in Quito you get nearly everything, but its not always easy to find
the laboratories which sell the chemicals and to get informations. Once I
had a very friendly meeting at the hospital Vozandes in Quito which is said
to be a good hospital and they for example get a lot of their chemicals from
