We are looking for volunteer doctors for our mission clinic

If you are a medical doctor with great idealism who wants to help really poor people, you have found the right place.

Commitment:  one month or more

You would stay in an inviting, modern and comfortable environment.
Room and board are free, but we can not affort to pay for transportation.

The mission clinic is very closely conected with the Catholic mission of Guadalupe. Therefore, we expect from you a Christian and missionary spirit or at the very least, great repect for other religions or cultures. We accept people from all religions.

Knowledge of the Spanish language is fundamental.

 

Dr. Serle Epstein, a volunteer doctor in our clinic writes:

"...In Ecuador Padre Jorge Nigsch has created an environment infused with creativity, innovation, and purpose. The populace has great medical and emotional needs. But the culture is rich. The fusion of European and Indian values intrigues me, When one sees what Padre is trying to do for his people, and how he is succeeding, the effort is infectious. I am drawn by a sense of being part of something larger than myself, and, since I am witnessing the beginning of the clinic, of using my skills to in a small way shape the health care environment of the Oriente for the future. Freed from other every day concerns it is the essence of service, almost religious in a religious environment. No matter how hard one works, one helps only a few villagers at a time. However one sets an example of professionalism for others who practice locally or who will follow. And of course there is the opportunity to help the person across from you, who in our community has many options even if poor, but in Ecuador would have no where else to turn. I have a romantic notion that the lack of compensation, and the difficulty of practice in that environment, may make the act of giving even more special and rewarding, a sense of purity of purpose, and sense of accomplishment and lack of commercialism. The Padre and Hermanas and villagers make it eminently clear that they are grateful. The people pay what they can for your service. All of this more than makes up for any frustration..."
 

Dr. Serle Epstein, a volunteer doctor in our clinic writes again in March 2004

 ....I wished to give an update on the progress of this three year old facility.  I have had the opportunity to volunteer on three occasions at this modern medical clinic. It is located on the grounds of a Catholic mission in the Ecuadorean Amazon, the Oriente, along the Peruvian border. There are examining rooms, dental suites, an operating theatre, a pharmacy, and a laboratory. Currently attending is a pediatrician from the United States who has been practicing general medicine for nine months, and will do so for another three. In addition there have been, and will continue to be, a steady stream of physicians coming for shorter stays, including internists, general practitioners, pediatricians, ophthalmologists and an otolaryngologist. There have also been physician assistants and advanced nurse practitioners. They have begun outreach programs and periodic mobile health fairs to even more remote locations. The facility serves an immediate population consisting of about three thousand persons in seven villages, but has remarkably been drawing patients from the entire provence of thirty thousand. Between the dentists, primary care physicians and surgeons, the average patient burden is about fifty visits each day and up to a staggering and overwhelming two hundred when surgury is performed. There is an opportunity for a volunteer primary care physician to work for several months to a year in addition to those who are able to come for a briefer sojourn. This might enable a certain amount of continuity of care supplemented by the efforts of other shorter term health care workers. Openings start late this coming summer into next year for internists, pediatricians, family and general practitioners, ophthalmologists, otolaryngologists and dentists. Room and board is free but travel is not reimbursed. Although a knowledge of Spanish is helpful there is a bilingual nurse on premise. Based at a Catholic mission, all faiths are welcome, but a respect for religion is important. ....... You may contact Padre Jorge Nigsch directly at nigsch57@easynet.net.ec   Thus far we have had volunteers from the United States, Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Irland, and Great Britain. .....
