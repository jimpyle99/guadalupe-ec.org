Ecuador inland trip:

Carriers that go directly from the USA to Quito are American Airlines and Continental. There are also others that go through Bogota in Colombia.

Our volunteer Dr. Serle Epstein describes it: "The international flight from New York to Quito is about $750 round trip. I land in Quito on a Friday night and spend a few hours resting with friends of Padre, Ernesto and Mercedes Proaños. They will give you a room and meals for $20 daily. Early the next morning I am back to the airport for the domestic flight to Catamayo, near Loja, about $140 round trip. I am either met at the small airport or will take a taxi to Loja, about $10 as I recall, and usually am met by one of the workers from the mission for the 3-4 hour trip across the Andes to the clinic. There is also a bus from the main terminal in Loja that would take you there. On the return trip I always build in an extra travel day, mostly to give flexibility in case the flight from Catamayo, in the mountains, is canceled for fear of
high winds. Usually this gives me one day to be a tourist and go to the indigenous markets around Quito. Again on the return I stay with Ernesto and Mercedes. Once you know your international flights let Padre Jorge know so that he can help arrange the local flights and accommodations."
