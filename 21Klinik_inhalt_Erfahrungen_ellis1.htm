   November - December 2002



 If you are thinking of volunteering at the clinic...

 

I spent seven weeks in late 2002 as a volunteer at the Clinica Misional "Nuestra Señora de Guadalupe".  It was the most interesting and challenging work I have ever done and I would recommend the experience without reservation to anyone looking for an opportunity to work in health care in a developing country.  The position provides an unusual combination of professional autonomy and institutional support.  An added bonus is the incredibly beautiful setting and comfortable living and working accommodations.  But the most thrilling part of my work in Guadalupe was getting to know and to care for my wonderful patients. On any given day I might see an infant with pneumonia, a woman with pelvic pain, and an elderly gentleman with post-herpetic neuralgia, not to mention countless patients complaining of low back pain and a variety of skin and gastrointestinal disorders. The people were very patient with my efforts to get to know them and their community, and accepted my presence among them with courtesy and warmth.  While I feel I did them some good, I was by far the greater beneficiary in my enhanced appreciation of the impact of poverty and underdevelopment on heath care.

 

If you are thinking of volunteering at the clinic, here are some personal qualities which will prove useful: curiosity, openness to new situations, tolerance of different beliefs, flexibility, lack of perfectionism, a positive attitude and a sense of humor. Important, although not essential, would be a good working knowledge of Spanish and some familiarity with and appreciation of Catholicism.  I would recommend that you immerse yourself in the life of the mission community, and visit the outlying barrios as much as possible to get a sense of the environment your patients come from. Bring lots of good books to occupy the long equatorial nights!  Lastly, I would urge you to use P. Jorge as a resource. His understanding of the people and local conditions were invaluable to me. 

 

I’d be happy to talk to anyone interested in volunteering.

 

Katherine Ellis, P.A.-C.
