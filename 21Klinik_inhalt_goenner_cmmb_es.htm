Catholic Medical Mission Board (CMMB) is the leading U.S.-based Catholic-sponsored philanthropic organization that focuses exclusively on providing healthcare to people in need worldwide.

A Catholic leader in international healthcare, CMMB has provided medicines and supplies to more than 100 countries around the world since 1928. More recently, our work has grown to include integrated healthcare programs, recruitment and placement of medical volunteers, training of local health practitioners, and emergency relief.

Today, we are focused on the development and management of healthcare programs that meet identified needs, make a lasting impact and utilize all our experience and expertise. Through these programs, we are helping to build a world where quality healthcare is available to all.

1913
Dr. Paluel Flagg, a young anesthesiologist at St. Vincent's Hospital in New York, visited Haiti and was struck by the plight of leprosy victims there. He became an active spokesperson for needy people around the world, recruiting missionaries for medical work in developing countries.


1928
Dr. Flagg inspired others to make the same commitment, and Catholic Medical Mission Board was formally established.

1931
CMMB purchased two Manhattan brownstones on West 17th Street. These buildings functioned as a combination office/warehouse for the organization.

1949
Thanks largely to the influence of Bishop Fulton J. Sheen, a CMMB director, pharmaceutical companies began donating medicines to CMMB. These medicines were repacked at the 17th Street headquarters and shipped to affiliate organizations in developing countries.

1960
CMMB's expanding services outgrew the headquarters building. The brownstones were demolished and new offices were built in their place.


1961
CMMB acquired a much-needed distribution center in nearby Long Island City. Today, it is a state-of-the-art facility with a computerized inventory management system and climate control.

1989
CMMB was the recipient of the prestigious Damian-Dutton Award for its work towards the eradication of leprosy.

2001
CMMB embarks on a new strategic course, focused on developing and managing integrated healthcare programs.
