September 2002

To Whom It May Concern,

I am writing this letter from the parish clinic in Guadalupe, province Zamora-Chinchipe, Ecuador. The clinic is new and was built and is maintained with donations from other countries. It is a rural clinic that offers much lacking health care to the primarily Shuar and Saraguro Indians that live in the surrounding area. There is a full time registered nurse and part time doctor working here as well as frequent visiting doctors and dentists who donate a few days, weeks or months of their time. We need more medicines to better help the people here. We need especially the following medicines and supplies:

  1. Vitamines for children, pregnant women, elderly and anemia
     
  2. 600 single dose parasite and ameba treatments
     
  3. 500 lice shampoo treatments
     
  4. Antimiotics (Topical, Oral and Vaginal)
     
  5. Antinflammatories, Antacids and Ulcer medications
     
  6. Sensor discs for Bayer Glucometer Style 3952H or visual color-change blood glucose test strips
     
  7. All types of ointment/soap/disinfectant for the skin
     
  8. A few Corticosteroids (injectable), muscle relaxants, antihistamines, topical antibiotics
     
  9. A couple of Stethoscopes  

We also hope to offer a much needed parasite medicine program to the 500 school children and to the 85 senor citizens in the immediate area. Thus, we need a donation of almost 600 single dose parasite medicine and if possible followed with a single dose ameba treatment, which is a very common condition here. Our other hope is to offer lice shampoo to all of the school children and vitamins to the elderly after their parasite medicine. Any help that you can offer us is very much appreciated.

Thank you for your time in considering this request and for your generosity.

Sincerely,

 P. Jorge Nigsch and nurse Amy Anderson
